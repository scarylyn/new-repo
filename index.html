Gamer picks

For the casual player

  • Ooblets

    A farming game, creature collector, and deck battler rolled into one! Ooblets is a great game for anyone who loves goofy characters, creature battling, home decoration, and farming. So much farming. What sets this game apart from other creature collectors is the fact that you grow your own creatures! You don't capture the creatures you battle, they give you a seed upon your victory and you take it home to grow your very own creature. Oh, and did we mention that the battles are dance battles?

    Glumberland

  • Pomofarm

    This game, while a game, is more of a tool. You set a focus timer to keep you on task, and at the end of a timer you're allowed a break. During focus time, you work on whatever you're working on in your daily life, and when the break time bell rings it's time to check on your duck! He's been hard at work farming! Water, harvest, and sell your crops, then it's back to focus time you go!

    Wormpod

  • Carmen Sandiego's Great Chase Through Time

    Where in time is Carmen Sandiego? A 1997 point and click game, Great Chase Through Time taught children history through the guise of hunting down the time travelling criminal Carmen Sandiego. Starting in Ancient Egypt, the ACME Agency has players hunt down criminals in 19 different time periods, while also getting a catchy theme song stuck in their heads.

    Broderbund

  • Logical Journey of the Zoombinis

    Another Broderbund classic, Logical Journey is at its core a learning game for children. Tasked with helping the small creatures called Zoombinis find a new home, children must solve a variety of puzzles based around the concepts of pattern recognition and player logic. Can you get all 1,250 Zoombinis to their new home?

    Broderbund

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.

Become a member today, and make sure to check out our Staff Picks section. This week's selection is full of books anyone interested in computer programming would love!
